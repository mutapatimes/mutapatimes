Moving to Zimbabwe · Connectivity

Zimbabwe SIM card & eSIM

Get your phone working from the moment you land. Physical SIMs from Econet, NetOne or Telecel require ID registration inside Zimbabwe; an eSIM bought before you fly skips the airport queue entirely. Compare Zimbabwe eSIM options below.

Zimbabwe eSIM — compare data plans

eSIM vs physical SIM

For most travellers, a Zimbabwe eSIM is the simpler option. You buy it online before you fly, install it on your phone, and it activates when you land. No queueing at OK Zim or a network shop with your passport for registration. Downsides: eSIM data is more expensive per GB than local prepaid, and you can't yet use eSIMs to register for EcoCash or other Zimbabwean mobile-money services.

A local physical SIM is cheaper and gives you a Zimbabwean number — useful if you'll be in the country for more than two weeks, want to use EcoCash or OneMoney, or need to register for anything that requires a local number (banking apps, ride-hailing). All three networks require passport ID registration at purchase; buy at the network shop, not from a street vendor.

What to do on the day you land

  1. If you bought a Zimbabwe eSIM before flying: activate it on the plane or once you land. You should have data the moment you exit the aircraft.
  2. If buying a local physical SIM: get through immigration first (you'll need USD cash for the visa at the desk), then head to the Econet or NetOne kiosk inside the arrivals hall or in town. Bring your passport for registration.
  3. Buy a starter bundle on the spot — the kiosks have prepaid voucher cards. Top up via *143# (Econet) or *124# (NetOne).
  4. For WhatsApp-heavy use, buy a WhatsApp bundle separately — it's much cheaper than the same data on a general internet bundle.

Roaming on your home SIM

Technically possible but expensive. UK carriers (EE, Three, Vodafone, O2) charge anywhere from £6 to £15 per MB in Zimbabwe with no daily caps. US carriers (T-Mobile, AT&T, Verizon) are similarly punitive — some offer "international day passes" at US$10/day with limited data. Australian and Canadian carriers vary widely.

South African carriers (Vodacom, MTN, Cell C, Telkom) typically offer roaming in Zimbabwe at more reasonable rates (R3–8 per MB) and Vodacom has a SADC roaming bundle worth checking. Still more expensive than a local SIM or eSIM.

Frequently asked questions

Can I buy a Zimbabwe SIM card before I travel?

Physical SIMs from Econet, NetOne or Telecel can only be bought inside Zimbabwe (passport registration required). The pre-travel option is a Zimbabwe eSIM — available online from Airalo, Holafly and others — which you install before flying and activate on landing.

Which Zimbabwean network is best?

Econet for widest coverage including rural areas. NetOne for cheaper data and city coverage. Telecel only if specifically priced lower. For travellers mostly in Harare, Bulawayo or major tourist sites, all three work.

How much does mobile data cost in Zimbabwe?

Local prepaid is US$5–15 for 1–3 GB. Tourist eSIMs are US$10–30 for 1–5 GB but more convenient. WhatsApp-only bundles are very cheap (US$1–3/week) and are the most-used product in Zimbabwe.

Can I use my UK / US / Australian SIM in Zimbabwe?

Yes via roaming but rates are punishing — often US$10+ per MB. A local SIM or eSIM is dramatically cheaper for anything beyond emergency texts.

Does WhatsApp work in Zimbabwe?

Yes — WhatsApp is the dominant messaging app, used for both personal and business. WhatsApp-only data bundles are the cheapest way to stay in touch.
