Zimbabwe’s economy, in numbers.

A living, quarterly portrait of a $30 billion diaspora-fed economy — what it sells, what it buys, where the money actually comes from, and what the published numbers quietly leave out.

Chapter 01 · GDP by sector

Where the money lives

…

The largest single contributor to the economy is the small-trade economy — spaza shops, market stalls, wholesalers — classified by ZimStat as Wholesale and retail trade. Mining is in the same league. Chapter 02 · Goods trade

The trade gap

…

For most of its modern history Zimbabwe imported far more than it exported. Then, in 2024, the goods trade balance quietly flipped — lithium, gold and tobacco exports finally outran the import bill. The shaded area below is the gap between the two lines, quarter by quarter, in millions of US dollars.

Chapter 03 · Remittances

The diaspora dividend

…

Personal transfers — money sent home by Zimbabweans living abroad — have grown into one of the country's largest reliable inflows. The official numbers below are the recorded transfers only; the true figure is widely believed to be higher once informal channels (cross-border traders, cash couriers) are counted.

Source: Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe / ZimStat, Balance of Payments — Personal Transfers.

Mining vs Agriculture

…

Chapter 05 · Sector composition

Services rising

…

Stacked over time, the top six sectors show a quieter trend: finance, ICT and wholesale trade are slowly taking share from manufacturing. Zimbabwe is becoming a services economy — not by design, but because the productive base is hollowing out under it.

Source: ZimStat, Quarterly Gross Domestic Product Tables. Top six sectors by latest-quarter contribution.

Chapter 06 · External balance

The missing money

…

Every Balance of Payments has a line called Net Errors and Omissions — the difference between what should add up and what actually does. In most economies it is small noise. In Zimbabwe's, it is a large, persistent figure: a quiet statistical admission that the informal economy moves real money the books cannot fully see.

Source: Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe, Balance of Payments — Current Account & Net Errors & Omissions.

METHODOLOGY

Every chart on this page is drawn from one of two open data sources: ZimStat's quarterly GDP tables, and the Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe's quarterly Balance of Payments. Both are public domain. ZimStat re-based the GDP series in 2024 Q1 following the introduction of the ZiG currency — pre- and post-2024 figures are not directly comparable, and any chart that crosses that line marks the discontinuity.
