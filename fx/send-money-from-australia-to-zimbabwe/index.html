How to send money to Zimbabwe in four steps

  1. Compare providers above. The cheapest by combined cost for typical retail amounts is currently Wise. Effective rate ~18.7218 ZWG per 1 AUD.
  2. Sign up with the provider. Australia regulators require ID verification (passport or driving licence) and a proof of address.
  3. Add your Zimbabwean recipient with their EcoCash or OneMoney number, a USD bank account number, or a cash-pickup ID. Mobile money is fastest; USD bank deposit is most flexible.
  4. Send. Enter the amount, confirm the rate and fee, and authorise the transfer. Most arrive within minutes (mobile money) to one business day (bank).

What currency should I send in?

The default and simplest is to send AUD and have the recipient receive USD in Zimbabwe — either to a USD bank account or as cash pickup. USD circulates freely alongside ZWG and most prices are still quoted in USD.

If your recipient specifically wants ZWG (Zim Gold), choose a provider that pays out to an EcoCash or OneMoney ZWG wallet. Note that the provider will convert at their own rate, not the parallel-market rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the cheapest way to send money from Australia to Zimbabwe?

Based on combined FX spread and flat fees, Wise is typically the cheapest for typical retail amounts: a 0.5% spread and a AUD 3.20 fee, paying out via USD bank deposit. For very small or very large amounts, run the calculator on the provider's own site as fee structures change at thresholds.

How long does it take to send money from Australia to Zimbabwe?

It depends on the provider and the payout method. Mobile money payouts (EcoCash, OneMoney) usually arrive in minutes. Cash pickup at branches (Mukuru, ZB Bank, OK Mart) is also minutes. USD bank deposits can take a few hours to one business day. Bank-to-bank transfers without using a remittance specialist can take 2–5 days and cost considerably more.

Is it legal to send money to Zimbabwe?

Yes. Australia has no restrictions on outbound remittances to Zimbabwe. Inbound remittances are encouraged by the Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe and are typically paid out in USD or via mobile money in ZWG.

Can I send to EcoCash or OneMoney from Australia?

Yes. WorldRemit, Mukuru and Sasai support EcoCash and OneMoney mobile-money payouts. Wise and Remitly typically only deposit to USD bank accounts. Check each provider's payout list for the current options.

What's the difference between USD and ZWG payout?

Most providers offer USD bank deposit as the default — this avoids any currency conversion at the Zimbabwean end. ZWG (Zim Gold) payouts convert at the provider's chosen rate, which may not match the official rate. For most diaspora purposes, USD payout is simpler.
