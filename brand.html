Typography

Two typefaces. No italics. Playfair Display for headlines and the wordmark; Inter for everything else — body, captions, data, navigation.

Playfair Display Black

Zimbabwe’s economy, in numbers.

Headlines, the wordmark and chapter titles. Tight tracking (-0.01 to -0.02em) at large sizes.

Inter Bold · Regular

For most things on this site, Inter does the work.

Body, captions, navigation, data labels. Bold for emphasis, Regular at 1.6–1.75 line height for reading.

06

Voice

How we sound across copy, captions, headlines and posts.

  • Plain English — short sentences, concrete nouns, no jargon unless we explain it.
  • Evidence-led — every number cites its source. ZimStat, RBZ, named reporting.
  • Quietly opinionated — we have a point of view but lead with the facts.
  • Diaspora-first — we explain Zimbabwe to Zimbabweans abroad, and Zimbabwe back to itself.
  • No italics. Emphasis through colour, weight or word order instead.

07

Do & don’t

Do

  • Keep the wordmark untouched. Don’t restretch, recolour or re-letter it.
  • Use the monogram for any application under 200px square.
  • Pair the brand red with ink and one paper tone, not multiple paper tones at once.
  • Keep at least the height of the “M” as clearspace around the wordmark.

Don’t

  • Don’t set the wordmark in italic, light, or any non-Playfair face.
  • Don’t use the old Great Zimbabwe ruins illustration as a logo. It’s retired.
  • Don’t use the wordmark over busy photos. Use the monogram instead.
  • Don’t introduce a second accent colour. The single red is the brand.
