For decades, economic growth has largely followed a linear logic: extract resources, manufacture products, sell them, and ultimately discard them. While this model powered industrial expansion worldwide, its structural weaknesses are becoming harder to ignore. Rising input costs, supply chain fragility, currency volatility, and environmental pressures are now central business concerns rather than distant risks.
