Victoria Falls is a small town built around one very large thing, and where you stay shapes how you experience it. The settlement is compact enough to walk, the rainforest and the bridge are at one edge of it, and the Zambezi runs along another, threading past a string of lodges before it reaches the lip. Some travellers want to be steps from the spray; others want a waterhole, a sundowner deck over the river, or the quiet of a private concession upstream.

This guide sorts the options by that choice. It also notes, honestly, which properties sit across the border on the Zambian side at Livingstone, because the booking feeds list them together and the two banks are a very different proposition at the immigration post.

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="By the falls" id="stay-town">
  <h2>In town, near the falls</h2>
  <p>The historic heart of Victoria Falls is the strip between the town and the bridge, and its anchor is The Victoria Falls Hotel, the Edwardian railway hotel that has stood since 1904 with its lawns running towards the gorge and a view of the bridge from the terrace. It is the grande dame of the whole region and worth a drink even if you sleep elsewhere.</p>
  <p>Close by, The Kingdom at Victoria Falls is the large, family-friendly resort near the rainforest entrance, and the Victoria Falls Rainbow Hotel is a solid full-service option in the town itself. For travellers on a tighter budget, Victoria Falls Backpackers and the N1 Hotel and Campsite cover the hostel and camping end, and 528 Victoria Falls Guest House is a smaller guest-house base within easy reach of the falls.</p>
</section>

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="Safari side" id="stay-safari">
  <h2>The safari lodges on the bush side</h2>
  <p>West of town, away from the river, a cluster of lodges trades the spray for the bush. The draw here is wildlife: the land runs into the national park, and elephants and buffalo move through it.</p>
  <p>Victoria Falls Safari Lodge is the best known, set on a plateau above a waterhole that draws game through the dry season, with the Safari Suites alongside for a more spacious stay. This is the side to choose if you want a sundowner watching animals drink rather than a walk to the rainforest gate.</p>
</section>

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="On the Zambezi" id="stay-river">
  <h2>On the Zambezi, upriver</h2>
  <p>Upstream of the falls, the Zambezi is calm and wide, and the lodges along it are built for the river: decks over the water, sunset cruises, the sound of hippo at night. This is the quiet, scenic end of the Victoria Falls offer.</p>
  <p>Victoria Falls River Lodge sits on the riverbank inside the national park, and Matetsi Victoria Falls occupies a large private concession further upstream for travellers who want space and exclusivity. The Victoria Falls Waterfront is the more accessible riverside option, with a range of rooms and a campsite, and a deck that makes the most of the same water.</p>
</section>

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="The Zambian side" id="stay-zambia">
  <h2>Across the bridge: the Zambian side</h2>
  <p>The falls are shared with Zambia, and the town of Livingstone on the far bank has its own accommodation. We flag these clearly because they are a border crossing away, not a short walk: a day trip between the two sides is easy, but you sleep in a different country.</p>
  <p>The Royal Livingstone Victoria Falls Zambia Hotel by Anantara is the marquee property on that side, set on the Zambezi within walking distance of the Zambian rim, and Victoria Falls Backpackers Zambia covers the budget end at Livingstone. Choose these if your itinerary is built around the Zambian bank or the Livingstone airport rather than the Zimbabwean town.</p>
</section>
