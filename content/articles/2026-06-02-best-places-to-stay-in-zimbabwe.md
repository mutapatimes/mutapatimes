Zimbabwe is a country of set pieces, and the trick to a good trip is matching where you sleep to which set piece you have come for. The capital is a working city of green suburbs; Victoria Falls is a small town built around one of the natural wonders of the world; Bulawayo is the unhurried heritage city and the door to the Matobo Hills; the east rises into cool mountains; the south holds the ruins that named the nation. Each asks for a different kind of bed.

This is our country-wide overview, drawn from the same Hotels.com inventory that powers our individual city guides. Use it to decide where to base yourself, then follow the links into each city for the neighbourhood-by-neighbourhood detail. The carousel at the end mixes stays from every region so you can browse the whole country in one place.

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="Harare" id="stay-harare">
  <h2>Harare: the gateway</h2>
  <p>Most trips begin in the capital, and the choice there is between the compact city centre, anchored by landmarks like the Monomotapa Hotel, and the leafy northern suburbs around Borrowdale, where the garden lodges and self-catering apartments sit. Stay central for meetings, stay north for space and quiet.</p>
  <p>Read the full guide: <a href="/articles/2026-06-02-where-to-stay-in-harare.html">Where to stay in Harare</a>.</p>
</section>

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="Victoria Falls" id="stay-vicfalls">
  <h2>Victoria Falls: the marquee</h2>
  <p>If there is one place every visitor wants to see, it is the falls, and the town around them has beds for every budget: the Edwardian grandeur of The Victoria Falls Hotel near the bridge, the game-rich plateau of Victoria Falls Safari Lodge on the bush side, and the quiet luxury of the lodges upriver on the Zambezi. The Zambian side at Livingstone is a border crossing away, and we flag those options clearly.</p>
  <p>Read the full guide: <a href="/articles/2026-06-02-where-to-stay-in-victoria-falls.html">Where to stay in Victoria Falls</a>.</p>
</section>

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="Bulawayo" id="stay-bulawayo">
  <h2>Bulawayo: heritage and the Matobo</h2>
  <p>The second city is the easiest to stay in, with wide avenues and a tier of characterful old hotels led by the Victorian Bulawayo Club and the mock-Tudor Cresta Churchill on the Matobo road. It is the natural base for a day among the granite and the rock art of Matobo National Park.</p>
  <p>Read the full guide: <a href="/articles/2026-06-02-where-to-stay-in-bulawayo.html">Where to stay in Bulawayo</a>.</p>
</section>

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="The east" id="stay-mutare">
  <h2>The Eastern Highlands: Mutare and the Vumba</h2>
  <p>For mountains, mist and pine, the east is the place to base yourself. Mutare is the border city and arrival point, with Holiday Inn Mutare for a practical night, while the real prize is up in the hills: the gateway lodge at Odzi and the cloud-forest stays of the Vumba.</p>
  <p>Read the full guide: <a href="/articles/2026-06-02-where-to-stay-in-mutare.html">Where to stay in and around Mutare</a>.</p>
</section>

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="The south" id="stay-masvingo">
  <h2>The south: Great Zimbabwe and Lake Mutirikwi</h2>
  <p>The dry-stone city of Great Zimbabwe is the country's greatest monument, and the best beds near it sit either beside the ruins, at the Great Zimbabwe Hotel, or out on Lake Mutirikwi at Norma Jeane's, in granite hills above some of the finest bass water in the region.</p>
  <p>Read the full guide: <a href="/articles/2026-06-02-where-to-stay-in-masvingo.html">Where to stay in and around Masvingo</a>.</p>
</section>

<section class="stay-section" data-stay-section data-stay-label="The Midlands" id="stay-gweru">
  <h2>The Midlands: Gweru and Antelope Park</h2>
  <p>Halfway between the two big cities, Gweru is mostly a stopover, but it has one stay worth planning around: Antelope Park, the private reserve outside town known for its lion conservation work. It is the Midlands stop that earns its night.</p>
  <p>Read the full guide: <a href="/articles/2026-06-02-where-to-stay-in-gweru.html">Where to stay in and around Gweru</a>.</p>
</section>
