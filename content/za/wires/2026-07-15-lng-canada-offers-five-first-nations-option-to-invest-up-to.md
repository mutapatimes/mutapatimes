LNG Canada said on Tuesday five First Nations will have an equity option to invest up to C$1-billion ($710.78-million) to acquire a storage tank planned to be built in a proposed expansion of the country's first major liquefied natural gas (LNG) export facility. First Nations, Canada's indigenous peoples groups, have been buying stakes in the country's energy projects seeking economic benefits from projects built on their land, amid rising capital requirements for companies in the sector.
