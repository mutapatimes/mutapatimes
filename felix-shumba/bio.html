Felix Shumba

b. 1985, Harare, Zimbabwe
Lives and works in Harare

Felix Shumba is an artist whose multidisciplinary practice spans painting, mixed media, and sculpture. Drawing on the rich visual traditions of Southern Africa and the material culture of Zimbabwe, Shumba creates works that interrogate the boundaries between memory and place, personal narrative and collective history.

His paintings — often large-scale, layered compositions in oil and acrylic — explore the tensions between abstraction and figuration, using colour and gesture to evoke the textures of landscape, urban life, and domestic space. In his mixed media works, Shumba incorporates found objects, textiles, soil, and organic materials, forging connections between the handmade and the industrial, the ancestral and the contemporary.

Shumba's sculptural practice centres on stone, metal, and wood, engaging with Zimbabwe's renowned tradition of stone sculpture while pushing toward new formal and conceptual territories. His three-dimensional works investigate the human figure, spiritual symbolism, and the weight of cultural inheritance.

His work has been exhibited internationally and is held in private collections across Africa, Europe, and North America.
