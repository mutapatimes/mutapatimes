What is news, and how to write a lede

Read first

News is what is new, true, and matters to your reader

A story is news when it is timely, close to home, and changes something for the people reading it. For The Mutapa Times that reader is a Zimbabwean, often in the diaspora, who wants the story told seriously rather than explained to outsiders.

News is not the same as public relations. A press release wants you to act or believe something. A news story tells you what happened and lets you decide. Holding that line is what makes a publication worth trusting.

Read first

The lede carries the whole story

The lede is the first sentence. It decides whether anyone reads the second. A strong lede leads with the most newsworthy fact, names who it affects, stays concrete, and gets out of the way. Aim for one clear idea, usually under 35 words, in plain active voice.

Weak ledes bury the point under throat-clearing: dates, process, or the name of a committee. Find the single thing a reader would tell a friend, and start there.

Write the lede

Write the opening sentence of a news story from these facts. Lead with what matters most. Keep it under 35 words, active voice.

  • Zimbabwe's Finance Minister has issued the country's first rules for cryptocurrency businesses.
  • Firms that buy, sell, or hold crypto must now register every year with the Financial Intelligence Unit.
  • The annual fee is US$500. Operating without registering is now a criminal offence.
  • It is a shift from the 2018 ban, which pushed crypto trading underground.
