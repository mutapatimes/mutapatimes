Good to know

The course

What is the Mutapa Times Academy?

A complete, self-paced online journalism course from The Mutapa Times. You learn the craft through interactive lessons and exams, earn a certificate, and can work towards a published byline.

Who is it for?

Anyone who wants to report and write: beginners, students, career changers, bloggers, and working writers who want to sharpen the fundamentals. No newsroom experience is needed.

Do I need any experience or qualifications to start?

None. It starts from the basics and builds all the way to a final exam and a real-world capstone.

What will I actually learn?

News values, the newsroom, reporting and interviewing, writing and structure, fact-checking, ethics, the business of news, how advertising and affiliate links work, working with the media and pitching, the elevator pitch, the press release, measuring impact, covering places and communities fairly, solution journalism, the digital journalist, getting hired and going independent. See the full syllabus.

How big is the course?

Eighteen units, 76 lessons and over 380 interactive exercises, with 16 checkpoint exams and a final. The Reading Room, where you read and analyse recent published articles, is compulsory before your certificate unlocks.

Is there a track for journalism or university students?

Yes. Alongside the Professional Certificate there is a University and Student track for journalism and media students in the UK and worldwide. It is the same course, plus a marked portfolio piece, CV-ready evidence of skills, and a reference from a working newsroom. Compare the tracks.

Are there live classes or deadlines?

No. It is fully self-paced, with no live sessions and no deadlines. Learn whenever it suits you.

How long does it take to finish?

Most people finish over a few weeks of steady study, but there is no time limit and you keep lifetime access.

What language is it in?

English.

Who teaches it?

It is self-paced, so you learn by doing rather than watching lectures. Our editors come in towards the end, to assess pitches and provide references.

Difficulty and exams

How hard is it?

It starts gentle and becomes genuinely demanding. You must pass each lesson at 80 percent, clear the checkpoint exams, and pass a 25-question final exam, so the certificate means something.

What happens if I fail an exam?

You simply review the material and retake it. There is no penalty and no limit on attempts.

Are the exams timed?

The checkpoint and final exams run with a countdown to keep you focused, but you can retake them as often as you need.

What is the capstone?

A real-world exercise where you take an actual press release and turn it into a fair, publishable story, step by step.

Your certificate, reference and byline

Is the certificate real?

Yes. It is a certificate of completion awarded by The Mutapa Times Academy, a working newsroom. It is our own credential, not a government or university accreditation. Its weight comes from the skills, the reference and the published clips behind it.

How do I get the certificate?

Finish the course and pass the final exam at the required mark. You can then download it or have it emailed to you.

Will it help my CV and applications?

That is the point. You leave with a credential, a reference and real published work, which is exactly what admissions officers and editors look for.

What is the written reference?

Strong graduates can receive a written reference from our editors, to use for university or job applications.

Do I really get published, with my own profile?

Finishing opens the contributor track. Graduates who pitch and publish with us earn a byline and a public profile page with their bio and stories. It depends on your work, but the path is real and it is what the course is built for.

Access and devices

Yes. Pay once and keep access for life. There is no subscription.

Yes. It works on any modern phone, tablet or computer through your browser. You need an internet connection to load the lessons.
