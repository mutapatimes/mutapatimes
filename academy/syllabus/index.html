Outcomes

What you will be able to do

Objectives are practical. By the end you should be able to:

  • Spot what makes a story newsworthy and tell news from opinion and PR.
  • Find sources, run an interview, and verify what you are told.
  • Structure a piece with the inverted pyramid and write clear, active copy.
  • Fact-check and copy-edit your own work to a publishable standard.
  • Understand how a newsroom runs and how to read any outlet critically.
  • Explain how news is funded, including how advertising and affiliate tracking work.
  • Pitch a story to the right journalist and deliver a sharp elevator pitch.
  • Write a press release that follows professional structure and the ABC of clear communication.
  • Read the modern audience: trust, news avoidance, platforms and paying readers.
  • Measure whether your work landed, from outputs to outcomes to impact.
  • Report solutions with evidence, not just problems.
  • Build a CV, a portfolio and a path to a real byline.

How it works

The shape of the course

Every lesson opens with short teaching cards, often with a chart, then a set of interactive exercises: multiple choice, sorting, matching, fill-the-gap, swipe and chart-completion tasks. You must pass each lesson at 80% before the next unlocks, so progress reflects mastery, not attendance.

Sixteen checkpoint exams test each unit, and a final exam tests the whole course. Written tasks are assessed against a fixed editorial rubric, and the capstone, a complete article, is read by a real editor.

The Reading Room is compulsory: you must read and analyse recent published articles before your certificate unlocks. Reading widely and critically is the single habit that most sharpens a reporter.

The modules

All 18 modules, in order

  1. Foundations

    Foundations

    What news is, how it differs from opinion and PR, voice, and the ethics that hold it all up.

  2. Foundations

    Inside the newsroom

    Who does what, how a story moves from tip to publication, and how to read an outlet.

  3. Foundations

    How news makes money

    Who pays for journalism, how paywalls work, and why funding shapes the news.

  4. Context

    Covering a continent fairly

    How coverage can stereotype a place, and how to tell fuller, fairer stories.

  5. Context

    A reporter's map

    Regions, media hubs, languages and the bodies that shape what gets reported.

  6. Reporting

    Reporting

    Find sources, run an interview, verify what you are told, and report from a distance.

  7. Writing

    Writing

    Structure a piece, open it well, write clearly, and edit your own copy.

  8. Writing

    Fact-checking and copy-editing

    The last lines of defence before a reader sees your work: verify, then catch every error.

  9. Publishing

    Pitch and publish

    Pitch a story properly, take edits well, and finish a publishable piece.

  10. Career

    The digital journalist

    Work across platforms, build a personal brand, and freelance with a portfolio.

  11. Career

    Getting hired

    Build a CV and cover letter that get a journalist noticed, and avoid the mistakes that get applications binned.

  12. Career

    Going independent

    Take everything you have learned and launch your own newsletter.

  13. Craft

    Solution journalism

    Report the problem and the evidenced response to it, while still scrutinising.

  14. New

    Working with the media world

    How journalists think, how to pitch a story, the elevator pitch, and the anatomy of a press release.

  15. New

    The business of news

    Who the audience really is, how advertising and affiliate links work, and how to measure whether your work landed. Includes a hands-on task: break down a live advert's link.

  16. Benchmark

    The realities of journalism jobs

    What the trade looks like in a mature media market: who does it, how they get in, and how trust is won.

  17. Craft

    Real-world critique

    Read real press releases, find the story, and avoid being a stenographer.

  18. Assessment

    Final assessment

    One comprehensive exam across the whole course. Pass it, complete the compulsory Reading Room, and earn your certificate.
