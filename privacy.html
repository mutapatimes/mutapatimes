Privacy Policy

Last updated: 13 May 2026 · Operator: The Mutapa Times, United Kingdom · Contact: news@mutapatimes.com

This Privacy Policy explains what personal information The Mutapa Times (“we”, “us”, the “Site”) collects when you use www.mutapatimes.com, how we use it, who we share it with, and what your rights are. We aim to keep this short, plain-English and accurate.

1. Who we are

The Mutapa Times is an independent business and intelligence newspaper operated from the United Kingdom. We are the data controller for any personal information collected through the Site under the UK GDPR.

2. What we collect

  • Email address if you subscribe to the newsletter or fill in a contact form.
  • Browser and device data (IP address, user agent, screen size, country, referrer) collected automatically by our analytics and tracking services when you visit the Site.
  • Reading behaviour (pages viewed, articles read, time on page, clicks, scroll depth) collected by analytics so we can understand which coverage matters to readers.
  • Comments and replies you send us by email or social media.

We do not ask for, store or process any payment information, government IDs, or sensitive personal data (health, religion, etc.).

3. How we use it

  • To deliver the newsletter — if you subscribe, we use your email solely to send you the briefings you signed up for, and you can unsubscribe with one click from any email.
  • To improve the Site — we use aggregated analytics to understand which articles, sections and features matter to readers so we can publish better content.
  • To respond to you — if you email us, we keep the thread so we can follow up.
  • To meet legal obligations — for example, retaining records where required by law.

4. Cookies & local storage

We use a small number of cookies and browser local-storage entries to:

  • Cache news data so the Site loads quickly on repeat visits.
  • Remember your “viewed” state on the stories rail so it doesn’t re-show stories you’ve already seen.
  • Power the analytics services described below.

You can clear cookies and local storage at any time from your browser settings. Doing so will reset your preferences and viewed-state but will not stop you using the Site.

5. Third-party services we use

We use the following services to run the Site and the newsletter. Each is a separate data controller for the information it processes on our behalf and has its own privacy policy.

  • GitHub Pages — hosts the Site (server logs may capture your IP address and request details). See GitHub’s privacy statement.
  • Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — aggregate audience analytics. See Google’s privacy policy.
  • Google Reader Revenue Manager & Google News (SwG) — surveys and reader-engagement features for news publishers. See Google’s privacy policy.
  • Google Fonts — serves brand typefaces; loaded by your browser when you visit. See Google’s privacy policy.
  • Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — stores subscriber email addresses and sends the newsletter. See Brevo’s privacy policy.
  • Contentsquare — UX analytics; aggregated behavioural signals (no payment data, no form-field capture). See Contentsquare’s privacy policy.
  • Metricool — lightweight site tracker for our social publishing. See Metricool’s privacy policy.
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging — powers optional breaking-news push notifications if you opt in. See Firebase’s privacy & security info.
  • YouTube — the Victoria Falls live cam is embedded from YouTube. See Google’s privacy policy.

6. How long we keep it

  • Newsletter emails: until you unsubscribe or ask us to delete them.
  • Analytics data: aggregated indefinitely; individual records retained per the service’s default (typically 14 months for GA4).
  • Inbound emails: retained while the conversation is active and for a reasonable period afterwards in case of follow-up.

7. Your rights

Under the UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Ask what personal data we hold about you.
  • Ask us to correct or delete it.
  • Withdraw consent (e.g. by unsubscribing from the newsletter) at any time.
  • Lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ico.org.uk).

To exercise any of these rights, email news@mutapatimes.com.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top of the page is the simplest way to tell if anything has changed. Material changes will also be announced in the newsletter.

9. Contact

Questions or requests about this policy:
The Mutapa Times — news@mutapatimes.com
